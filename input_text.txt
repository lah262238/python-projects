Prompt engineering has rapidly evolved from an informal practice into one of the most in-demand technical skills in the AI industry. Whether you are building intelligent customer support systems, AI-powered research tools, autonomous agents, or creative applications, your ability to communicate effectively with large language models (LLMs) determines the quality, reliability, and usefulness of everything you create.
This book is designed as a complete, self-contained curriculum for anyone who wants to master prompt engineering — particularly those building AI-powered products and applications. It assumes no prior experience with machine learning, but does assume familiarity with basic programming concepts. By the end, you will have both theoretical understanding and practical skills to write, test, iterate, and deploy prompts in real production environments.
Each chapter builds on the previous one. Code examples use Python with the Anthropic Claude API, but the principles apply equally to OpenAI GPT, Google Gemini, and any modern LLM. Where techniques differ across models, we note the differences explicitly.
One piece of advice before you begin: do not just read this book. Run every example. Break every prompt deliberately. The fastest path to expertise is hands-on experimentation.
